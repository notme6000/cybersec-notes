# Types of Hackers (With Examples)

## 1. White Hat Hackers

White hat hackers are ethical security professionals who test systems to find and fix vulnerabilities legally.

**Purpose:**

* Improve security
* Perform penetration testing
* Help organizations prevent cyberattacks

**Example:**
Security researchers working with companies like Google to report bugs through bug bounty programs.



## 2. Black Hat Hackers

Black hat hackers break into systems illegally for personal gain, revenge, or malicious intent.

**Purpose:**

* Steal data
* Spread malware
* Demand ransom

**Example:**
Cybercriminal groups behind ransomware attacks on hospitals and businesses.



## 3. Grey Hat Hackers

Grey hat hackers operate between white and black hats. They may access systems without permission but usually do not have malicious intent.

**Purpose:**

* Expose security flaws
* Sometimes request payment after finding vulnerabilities

**Example:**
A hacker who breaks into a website without permission and then informs the owner about the weakness.



## 4. Script Kiddies

Script kiddies are inexperienced individuals who use ready-made hacking tools created by others.

**Purpose:**

* Show off
* Cause minor disruption
* Experiment without deep knowledge

**Example:**
Using pre-built DDoS tools downloaded from the internet.



## 5. Hacktivists

Hacktivists use hacking to promote political or social causes.

**Purpose:**

* Protest
* Spread political messages
* Target government or corporate websites

**Example:**
Anonymous, known for website defacements and cyber protests.



## 6. State-Sponsored Hackers

These hackers work for governments to conduct cyber espionage or cyber warfare.

**Purpose:**

* Spy on other countries
* Steal military or economic secrets
* Disrupt critical infrastructure

**Example:**
Groups allegedly linked to national intelligence agencies conducting cyber espionage operations.



## 7. Cyber Terrorists

Cyber terrorists use hacking to create fear or disrupt essential services.

**Purpose:**

* Attack power grids
* Disrupt banking systems
* Create panic

**Example:**
Attempted attacks on critical infrastructure systems.



## 8. Insider Hackers

Insider hackers are employees or trusted individuals who misuse authorized access.

**Purpose:**

* Steal company data
* Leak confidential information
* Damage systems

**Example:**
An employee copying confidential company files before leaving a job.
