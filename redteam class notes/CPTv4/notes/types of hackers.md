## 1. White Hat Hackers (Ethical Hackers)

- Also called ethical hackers or penetration testers
- Work legally to find security vulnerabilities
- Help organizations improve cybersecurity
- often certified (CEH, OSCP, etc)

- _Legal_

## 2. Black Hat hackers

- Break into systems illegally
- Steal data, spread malware, demand ransom
- Motivated by money, revenge, or disruption


- _Illegal_
- _Malicious intent_

## 3. Gray Hat Hackers

- Between white and black hats
- may hack without permission but not for personal gain
- often expose vulnerabilities publicly

- _can be illegal but not always malicious_

## 4. Script kiddies

- inexperienced hackers
- use ready-made tools or scripts created by others
- usually lack deep technical knowledge


## 5. Hacktivists

- hack for political or social causes
- deface websites or leak information
- motivated by ideology rather than money

## 6. State-Sponsored Hackers

- work for government
- conduct cyber-espionage or cbyerwarfare
- target other countries or organizations

## 7. Insider Threats

- employees or contractors
- abuse legitimate access to steal or damage data
- can be intentional or accidental

## 8. Cyber Terrorists

- Aim to create fear or disrupt critical infrastructure
- Target power grids, hospitals, financial systems
