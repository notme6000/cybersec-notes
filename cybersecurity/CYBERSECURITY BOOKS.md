

### **1. The Basics of Hacking and Penetration Testing** – _Patrick Engebretson_

- Best for: Absolute beginners
- Covers: Step-by-step penetration testing, Linux basics, Metasploit, Nmap, and password cracking.
- Why? It’s practical, easy to follow, and great for those new to ethical hacking.

### **2. The Hacker Playbook 3: Practical Guide to Penetration Testing** – _Peter Kim_

- Best for: Beginners who want hands-on experience
- Covers: Real-world attack techniques, penetration testing methodologies, and red team tactics.
- Why? It teaches hacking through real-life simulations.

### **3. Hacking: The Art of Exploitation** – _Jon Erickson_

- Best for: Learning the mindset of a hacker
- Covers: Programming, shell scripting, buffer overflows, and cryptography.
- Why? Great for understanding hacking concepts deeply.

### **4. Penetration Testing: A Hands-On Introduction to Hacking** – _Georgia Weidman_

- Best for: A structured introduction to ethical hacking
- Covers: Kali Linux, Metasploit, wireless security, and social engineering.
- Why? A beginner-friendly, hands-on approach with practical exercises.

### **5. Cybersecurity for Beginners** – _Raef Meeuwisse_

- Best for: A broad introduction to cybersecurity
- Covers: Cyber threats, risk management, security concepts, and how hacking works.
- Why? Ideal for those looking for a general overview of cybersecurity.
